What a water hyacinth basket is

Water hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) is a floating freshwater plant with round leaves and lilac flowers. It grows aggressively across the Mekong Delta in southern Vietnam, forming dense mats on rivers, canals, and shrimp ponds. Left alone it clogs waterways and starves fish habitats of oxygen. Harvested, dried, and twisted into rope, it becomes the raw material for one of Vietnam's largest handicraft export categories.

To make a basket, artisans cut the stems close to the waterline, tie them into bundles, and sun-dry them for three to five weeks depending on weather. The dried stems are then split, twisted, or braided into ropes ranging from thin cord to thumb-thick cable. Those ropes are woven over a shape-holding frame — a bent metal wire on a laundry hamper, a rattan hoop on a round nesting basket, or a wooden ring on a magazine holder. Finishing is a series of quiet steps: trim, brush, oven-dry to below fifteen percent moisture, treat against mould, and cool before packing.

The finished basket is light, breathable, and warm-toned. It sits at a lower price point than rattan or wicker, holds shape better than seagrass under load, and takes buyer-specified dyes and washes cleanly. Retail buyers select water hyacinth for storage baskets, laundry hampers, nursery bins, planter holders, magazine baskets, gift hampers, tray sets, and low-decor floor pieces. E-commerce buyers add utility SKUs like nested sets and stackable cubes.

Types of water hyacinth baskets

The Storage Baskets Collection 2026 organises HMD Hycraft's water hyacinth range into eight functional categories. Most retail programmes draw from two or three of these categories to give shoppers a coherent shelf story.

  • Storage baskets and nested sets. The volume category. Square, rectangular, oval, and round shapes in single-piece or nested-set formats (usually sets of two, three, or four). Common lids: open top, cotton-lined open top, cloth-flap closure. Best for closet organisation, playroom shelves, and open-shelving retail displays.
  • Laundry hampers. Taller pieces with a wire or bamboo internal frame and often a lift-off lid. Two-piece sets sell well in North American bathroom programmes. Interior cotton liners protect delicates.
  • Nursery and toy storage. Softer weaves, rounded edges, and lower profile heights matched to shelf systems from Ikea, Kallax, and similar. Retailers who ship children's ranges appreciate the anti-mould treatment being non-toxic.
  • Planter baskets and cachepots. Cachepot sleeves that fit standard nursery pot sizes (four, six, eight, ten, twelve inches). Water hyacinth's tolerance of humidity makes it a strong fit for the indoor-plant market.
  • Magazine and floor baskets. Tall vertical formats with rounded openings, sometimes with a folded lip. Used as a hallway umbrella stand, a fireplace kindling holder, or a magazine caddy next to a reading chair.
  • Trays and shallow bowls. Rectangular and oval trays with woven handles. Popular in gifting programmes as a base under artisan food or wellness kits.
  • Gift hampers. Structured hampers with a hinged lid, ribbon-tie closures, and a cotton or gingham interior lining. Widely used across European Christmas programmes and Latin American cesta gifting.
  • Home decor pieces. Non-utility SKUs — decorative floor pieces, lantern-shape hurricanes, wall baskets, plant hangers. These sit above the utility range on price and open the door to a fuller room story.

All eight categories are available in the natural water-hyacinth colour and in a small range of dye washes — brown wash, white wash, black, and a warm charcoal grey. Custom colourways are quoted per project against a Pantone reference.

Sustainability and the invasive-species story

Water hyacinth is one of the world's most aggressive invasive aquatic weeds. It doubles its biomass in eight to twelve days, forms mats several metres thick, blocks navigation and fishing routes, and starves fish habitats of dissolved oxygen. Governments across South-East Asia, Africa, and Latin America spend serious money each year attempting to control it.

Harvesting water hyacinth for craft simultaneously solves two problems: it removes the pest and it supports rural livelihoods. In Đồng Tháp Province alone, the weaving cooperatives HMD works with employ several thousand women, many working from home. The plant regrows within weeks, so there is no depletion risk on the input side and no reason to switch to synthetic substitutes. The finished basket is 100 percent biodegradable — when it eventually retires, it composts back into the soil rather than sitting in landfill for four hundred years.

From a carbon perspective, the material is close to neutral. The plant absorbs CO₂ during growth. Drying is powered by tropical sunlight rather than gas or grid electricity. Ocean freight is the largest carbon line in the finished landed cost, but a full container of natural-fibre baskets displaces several containers of plastic storage bins that would otherwise be shipped from petrochemical-heavy supply chains. Retail buyers who care about scope-3 emissions can point to a defensible story here without greenwashing.

Quality control and supply chain

HMD Hycraft is an export intermediary, not a factory. Handwoven water hyacinth is a cottage craft — no single factory produces it at industrial scale. What HMD provides is quality control, export compliance, and logistics across a trusted network of BSCI-audited weaving cooperatives in Đồng Tháp Province. That intermediary role is deliberate: it lets buyers place one order and receive one container of consistent goods without having to build a supplier network of their own inside the Mekong Delta.

Where the quality risk sits

Three risks matter for a buyer moving water hyacinth baskets:

  • Moisture and mould. Baskets dispatched above 15 percent internal moisture will grow mould in the container during the 30 to 45 days of ocean transit. HMD dries every unit at HCMC and applies an eco-friendly anti-mould treatment. Buyer's inspection at the DC should find zero mould claims across a container.
  • Dimensional drift. Handwoven work has natural tolerance. HMD holds tolerance at ±5 percent on height and diameter against the approved sample; anything above that is rejected at the packing stage.
  • Weave discipline. Loose or uneven weave is a village-level issue. HMD's field team visits partner workshops during production to catch drift early. A retail buyer who cares about visual consistency can nominate a third-party auditor (SGS, Bureau Veritas, or the buyer's own team) at any stage; audit fees are on the buyer's account.

Compliance and certification

Water hyacinth baskets classify under HS 4602 ("plaiting materials, other than of vegetable materials"). Phytosanitary certificates and Certificate of Origin (Form A, Form B, Form E, or Form EUR.1 depending on destination) are issued through Vietnam Customs before B/L release. Anti-mould treatment is compliant with import regulations for the EU, UK, US, Canada, Australia, Japan, and South Africa. Workshop partners are BSCI-audited; SEDEX and BRC-Consumer Products audits can be arranged through nominated third parties.

Wholesale essentials: MOQ, lead time, container maths

The practicals of moving a container are usually where a first buyer wants numbers, not narrative. Here is the short version.

Rule of thumb. A 40-foot high-cube container carries 2,200 to 3,000 water hyacinth baskets in a nested-set programme, or 1,400 to 1,900 in a mixed programme of larger single pieces (hampers, floor baskets). Deposit 30 percent, balance 70 percent before B/L release, T/T. Lead time 45 to 60 days from PO and approved samples. FOB HCMC (Cat Lai or Cai Mep). Freight is the buyer's forwarder unless CIF or DAP is specifically quoted.

MOQ

Standard SKUs from the Storage Baskets Collection 2026 start at 300 to 500 units per SKU. Mixed containers with several SKUs are welcomed and often more practical for a first order. Custom sizes and colourways add a sampling window of two to three weeks.

Lead time

Standard lead time is 45 to 60 days from confirmed PO and approved pre-production samples. That includes cottage drying (weather-dependent), weaving, framing, kiln-drying to below 15 percent moisture, anti-mould treatment, packing, and container consolidation at HMD's HCMC facility. Peak-season windows (August to November) may extend by 10 to 15 days as village capacity peaks.

Container maths

A 40'HQ container has an internal volume of roughly 76.4 m³. Nested set programmes ship efficiently because the smaller pieces sit inside the larger ones — a set-of-3 nested basket takes only slightly more volume than the largest piece alone. Single-piece programmes (laundry hampers, floor baskets) fill the container faster on volume but often before hitting weight limits. HMD provides a container load plan with cartons per SKU, palletisation option, and pre-shipment photos before B/L is issued.

Payment

Standard terms are 30 percent deposit against proforma invoice and 70 percent balance before B/L release, by T/T. For repeat buyers, L/C at sight or open terms can be discussed on a case-by-case basis. Invoicing is in USD or EUR. HMD does not accept cryptocurrency for wholesale invoices.

Care and maintenance for the end consumer

The retailer's care card can lift much of this straight into the box. Water hyacinth baskets look after themselves if the end consumer follows a short list of do's and don'ts.

  • Keep dry, ventilated, and out of prolonged direct sunlight. Sunlight will fade the natural colour over months; occasional exposure is fine.
  • Wipe with a soft dry cloth or a lightly damp cloth if soiled. Never submerge in water and never machine wash. Standing water will loosen the weave.
  • Keep away from radiators, ovens, and permanently humid corners (behind toilet cisterns, bathroom floor near a shower).
  • If a basket feels slightly damp after a wet season, air it in a shaded outdoor spot for a few hours until dry.
  • Use a cotton or linen liner for laundry hampers if soiled damp clothes will sit inside for hours.
  • A gentle spray of vinegar-and-water once a year discourages mould in high-humidity homes.

With basic care, a water hyacinth basket lasts five to ten years of daily use and takes on a gently darker patina over time.

Frequently asked questions

What is a water hyacinth basket?

A handwoven basket made from the dried stems of the water hyacinth plant. Fast-growing, freshwater, aquatic. Vietnam's Mekong Delta is the leading source. Handwoven over a metal or rattan frame, then dried and treated for export.

Where do the best water hyacinth baskets come from?

Vietnam's Mekong Delta — Đồng Tháp, An Giang, and Kiên Giang provinces — is the leading global source. Longer, more uniform fibres and tighter weave discipline than Indonesian or Philippine alternatives. HMD Hycraft sources exclusively from BSCI-audited cooperatives in Đồng Tháp.

What is the MOQ for wholesale water hyacinth baskets?

MOQ starts at 300 to 500 units per SKU on standard designs. Mixed containers with several SKUs are welcomed. Custom sizes or colourways may require larger MOQ for sampling economics.

How long does production take?

Standard lead time is 45 to 60 days from PO and approved samples. Peak-season (August to November) may add 10 to 15 days.

Are water hyacinth baskets eco-friendly?

Yes. Water hyacinth is an invasive weed — harvesting it removes an ecological pest and supports rural livelihoods. The finished basket is 100 percent biodegradable and uses no petrochemical inputs.

How do these compare to seagrass, rattan and bamboo?

Water hyacinth is softer, warmer, and cheaper per container. Seagrass is coarser and cooler. Rattan is harder and better for fine patterns. Bamboo is the hardest and best for rigid frames. Most retail programmes mix two or three materials.

How should end-consumers care for the basket?

Keep dry and ventilated. Wipe with a soft cloth. Never submerge. Air-dry if damp. Five to ten years of daily use is realistic with basic care.
