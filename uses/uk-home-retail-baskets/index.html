Handwoven Baskets for UK Home Retail — Wholesale Supplier from Vietnam

Mixed storage, laundry, and bathroom programmes sized for UK interior retail chains and mail-order catalogues. Container discipline, marketing photography, and Form A GSP paperwork included.

A UK category manager evaluating a natural-fibre basket supplier is really testing three things: whether one supplier can hold breadth across storage, laundry, and bathroom without splitting the PO across three vendors; whether the container consolidation discipline holds up at Felixstowe or Southampton; and whether the marketing photography lands in the catalogue on time. This page is a shortlist for exactly that brief.

What buyers ask for on this category

  • One supplier for a mixed basket programme. Storage, laundry, and bathroom in the same container so the category-management team briefs one vendor, not three. HMD's Storage Baskets Collection 2026 covers 74 designs across the working categories.
  • Form A GSP for zero-duty entry. Every HMD invoice for UK-bound shipments includes a Form A certificate of origin issued through Vietnam Customs. Buyers under the UK Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS) clear at zero duty on HS 4602 handwoven basketwork.
  • Marketing photography ready for the catalogue. Every SKU ships with hi-res product photography on a neutral background plus at least one styled lifestyle image. Full-programme campaigns quote a small marketing shoot fee for buyer-directed environments.
  • Container discipline at Felixstowe and Southampton. HMD consolidates at HCMC and provides a load plan, cartons-per-SKU count, palletisation notes, and photo/video documentation of the load. Container ETA to Felixstowe is typically 34 to 42 days from B/L; Southampton 32 to 40 days.

Container maths

A first UK programme container typically consolidates five to seven SKUs across storage, laundry, and bathroom. A 40-foot high-cube holds 1,100–1,400 nested sets plus 250–300 tall pieces (laundry, magazine) when packed correctly. Exact CBM per carton is confirmed against the exact items in your enquiry so the container load plan matches the buyer's DC unload schedule.

Frequently asked

Do you supply UK retail chains directly, or through an agent?
Both. HMD works direct with UK interior retail chains and also supplies through UK-based sourcing agents. Direct buyers get a small pricing benefit; agent buyers get the local UK admin layer. Either route uses the same production line and QC standard.
Do you meet BRC or SEDEX audit requirements?
HMD's workshop network is BSCI-audited. Buyers requiring a BRC-Consumer Products or SEDEX audit can nominate a third-party auditor (SGS, Bureau Veritas) and HMD arranges the workshop visit; audit fees are on the buyer's account. Any additional buyer-specific compliance requests are welcomed at first-order stage.
What is the container ETA to a UK port?
Approximate transit from HCMC B/L date: Felixstowe 34–42 days, Southampton 32–40 days, London Gateway 34–42 days. Actual ETA depends on carrier service and seasonal port congestion. HMD provides the B/L number as soon as issued for buyer tracking.
Can you deliver direct to our UK DC?
HMD's default incoterm is FOB Ho Chi Minh City; the buyer's freight forwarder handles UK entry. CIF and DAP UK DC are quoted on request — DAP requires the buyer's EORI number for HMRC clearance. Consolidated DAP to a Midlands or Kent DC adds roughly 8–14 days on top of ocean transit.
Do you supply marketing images for our catalogue?
Yes. Every SKU ships with a hi-res product photo on a neutral background and at least one styled lifestyle image. Buyer-specific marketing shoots (particular room set, buyer-branded props) are quoted as a separate line item and typically add two to three weeks.
What is the lead time from PO to FOB HCMC?
Standard designs from the Storage Baskets Collection ship in six to eight weeks from confirmed PO. Custom sizes or a new colourway add two to three weeks for sampling. Container consolidation adds three to five working days after production is complete.
