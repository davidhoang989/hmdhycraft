From wild riverside to living rooms across the world

14 March 2026·2 min read

I still remember the first time I saw water hyacinth growing wild by the riverside. It looked simple. Almost unnoticed.

Today, seeing it transformed into finished products like these, I feel something very different.

Because behind each piece, there is not just material. There are hands. There is time. There is patience.

Many of these products are woven by women who, in their daily lives, take care of their families, their homes, and still dedicate hours to crafting each detail.

From raw, natural fibres to something that can be placed in a living space halfway across the world, it is not just production. It is transformation.

And honestly, there is a quiet kind of happiness in witnessing that journey.

Sometimes the value of a product is not in how it looks. It is in the story it carries.
