The cheapest supplier is usually the most expensive one

14 June 2026·2 min read

Most home décor buyers ask the same question first: "How much per piece?" Fair question. Wrong place to start.

Here is what switching suppliers every season actually costs you.

  • Repeated sampling. Every new workshop starts from zero on your specs.
  • Packaging guesswork. A supplier who does not know your carton marks ships you crushed corners.
  • Drift between batches. Size, shape, and colour tone wander when nobody remembers last order's notes.
  • Peak-season delays. Start sourcing in high season and you are at the back of a long line.

What a long-term partner gives you instead

  • They keep your size notes, packing methods, and carton marks on file. Reorders move faster, with fewer misunderstandings before mass production.
  • They know which sizes sell in your market, and can warn you when a new design will not survive bulk production.
  • They prep materials before your season hits, instead of scrambling when you do.

The point was never the lowest price. It is fewer repeated mistakes and steadier quality you can plan around.

So before you choose a handwoven basket supplier, ask one more question:

"Can this partner support my brand next year, not just this order?"
